Conclusion: A Restoration, Not a Replacement

The Book of Moses presents itself as a restored and expanded version of the Genesis account. It maintains the ancient narrative and structural framework found in the Torah portion of Bereshit while adding significant theological context, dialogue, and detail, particularly through the visionary prelude of Moses 1 and the extensive teachings of Enoch. The parallel is not one of simple copying, but of a re-envisioning of a shared foundational story.
